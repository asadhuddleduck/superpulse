## Body (4 paragraphs)

> Look. Before I tell you anything about me, let me tell you what I know about you. You're tired. You've got a business with empty chairs in it right now, and every one of those empty chairs is costing you money you don't have. You've tried boosting posts. You've tried the nephew who "knows Instagram." You've tried that agency that took a grand and sent you a PDF. None of it moved the needle. And you're on this page because some part of you still believes the right person, doing the right thing, could fill those chairs by Friday. Good. Keep believing that. Because that person exists and I'm about to tell you how I got here.

> I was going to be an astrophysicist. My dad was a doctor, and would have been over the moon. He woke at 4am to do the work before the work, and I grew up watching that, so I do the same. Then one day I dropped out. Flew far from home. Spent three years on marble floors with books older than most European capitals. The kind of quiet work most people don't have the stomach for.

> When I came back, I wasn't trying to start a company. I was paying rent. I ran ads for small businesses after my day job, expecting nothing in particular. Then one launch pulled 4,000 people. A single-location brand became five. A new shop hit 12,000 followers before it even opened. I couldn't keep ignoring it, so I built Huddle Duck.

> Marble floors and star charts led here, and I still can't fully explain the path. But I think a brain trained in both scientific rigour and spiritual discipline just sees patterns other people miss.
