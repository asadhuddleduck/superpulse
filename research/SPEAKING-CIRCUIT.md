## SECTION 2: HOW TO STRUCTURE A TALK THAT CONVERTS

### Optimal Talk Length

| Length | Best For | Conversion Potential |
|--------|----------|---------------------|
| **10 min** | Lightning talks, expo stages, pitch events | Low — too short for trust-building |
| **20 min** | Conference breakout sessions (brightonSEO standard) | SWEET SPOT — enough for transformation moment + CTA |
| **45 min** | Keynotes, workshops | High if you're already known; risky if first-time |

**Recommendation:** Target 20-minute slots. This is the standard at brightonSEO, B2B expos, and most UK marketing conferences. The 10/20/30 rule (10 slides, 20 minutes, 30pt font minimum) is widely used.

### The SuperPulse Talk Framework (Adapted from Daniel Priestley)

**Title:** "How I Replaced My Own Agency with an AI Ad Engine (And Why You Should Too)"

**Structure (20 minutes):**

1. **HOOK (0–2 min):** "I used to charge restaurants £3,000/month to run their Meta ads. Now an AI does it better for £497 one-time. Here's what happened."

2. **THE PROBLEM (2–5 min):** Paint the pain. Restaurant owners paying agencies thousands with no transparency. Show a real before/after from your 8 legacy clients. Use specific numbers (ROAS, spend, revenue).

3. **THE SHIFT (5–8 min):** The market change. AI can now do what agencies charged for. Reference the broader trend (ChatGPT, Claude, Meta Advantage+). Position SuperPulse within this wave.

4. **THE MINI-TRANSFORMATION (8–15 min):** THIS IS THE CONVERSION MOMENT. Show a live demo or case study. Walk through exactly what SuperPulse does. The audience should think "this could work for me." Use screenshots, real data, specific metrics.

5. **THE FRAMEWORK (15–17 min):** Give them a takeaway they can use TODAY even without SuperPulse. E.g., "The 3-Signal Ad Test" — three things to check before spending £1 on Meta ads. This builds goodwill and positions you as generous, not salesy.

6. **THE CTA (17–20 min):** "If you want to try this yourself, scan this QR code to join the SuperPulse waitlist. Early access is free for the first 500 businesses." Natural, not pushy.

### QR Code Strategy

| Placement | Effectiveness | Notes |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Every slide | LOW — banner blindness | Distracting, looks desperate |
| Last slide only | MEDIUM | Works if people take photos of slides |
| **Last 3 slides + stay on screen during Q&A** | **HIGH** | QR visible during the highest-engagement moment (Q&A) |
| Physical handout/card on seats | HIGH | People take it with them. Cost: ~£0.05/card |

**Best practice:**
- Use a DYNAMIC QR code (edit destination without reprinting)
- Minimum 5cm × 5cm for large rooms
- High contrast (dark code on light background)
- Clear CTA text: "Join the waitlist — scan here" (6 words max)
- Link to: superpulse.ai/waitlist (or similar) with UTM tracking per event
- QR resolves to a mobile-optimised page (not a full website)

### Expected Conversion Rates

| Scenario | Attendee → Scan | Scan → Signup | Net Rate |
|----------|----------------|---------------|----------|
| First few talks (unknown speaker) | 8–12% | 60–70% | **5–8%** |
| After 5+ talks (building reputation) | 15–25% | 70–80% | **12–20%** |
| Workshop/hands-on format | 25–40% | 80–90% | **20–35%** |

**Example:** 200-person conference room × 10% conversion = 20 waitlist signups per talk. At 2 talks/month = 40 signups/month from speaking alone.
